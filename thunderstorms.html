THUNDERSTORMS

Be Prepared for Anything

What are Thunderstorms?

All thunderstorms produce lightning. Thunderstorms are only considered severe if it produces at least 1-inch diameter hail or at least 58 mile per hour wind. Thunderstorm watches mean there is a possible severe storm in the area. Warnings mean there has been a storm spotted in the area. They can be spotted by spotters or radar. Some thunderstorms can also produce tornadoes and other forms of severe weather.

Despite the warnings some people are still seriously injured or killed as a result of thunderstorms each year. While people do get seriously injured from lightning strikes, most people survive getting hit by lightning.

Prepare Early- a list of things to do.

Before-
  1. It is recommended that you have an emergency toolkit.
  2. Secure outdoor objects that could cause damage.
  3. Shutter windows (or close window blinds and curtains) and secure outside doors.
  4. Unplug electronics if you know a storm is coming.
During-
  1. Get inside a home, building, or hard top car (you are much safer even if lightning strikes it)
  2. Avoid contact with electronics that are plugged in.
  3. Avoid contact with plumbing since it can conduct electricity.
  4. Avoid windows and doors.
  5. Avoid sheds and trees that are on their own.
  6. Avoid metal object.
  7. If on the road try to safely park and turn on your flashers until the heavy rain ends.
After-
  1. If you or someone you know is injured by lightning call 911. They need medical attention as soon as possible.
  2. Check the injured for burns or other injuries.
  3. If they are not breathing normally then begin CPR. If they are care for them as needed.

Make a plan with your family so that if you get separated you know where to meet.

Survive in the Moment and Aftermath

  • Tip 1- Know what to do in each disaster. Try and have at least one family memeber be trained in first aid so you can help any injured person. Watch your animals closely after a storm to make sure they don't get hurt.
  • Tip 2- Don't drive on flooded roads, you never know what is going on underneath the water. You also never know how deep the water is.
