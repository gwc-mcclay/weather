TORNADOES

Mother Nature's Rage

What are Tornados?

Tornadoes can happen anywhere at anytime. But these wonderous weather occourances mostly happen in a wide area of the United States that is known as Tornado Alley which consists of South Dakota, Colorado, Nebraska, Kansas, Texas and the most Tornado prone state is Oklahoma which gets the most severe tornadoes for some reason.

Most Tornadoes form from extreamly severe Thunderstorms, but don't worry, that doesn't happen with all thunderstorms. To create a tornado, you need warm, moist air from the Gulf of Mexico, and cool, dry air from the north and Canada. When these two air masses meet, they create and intability in the atmosphere.

How to survive

It is often said that Tornadoes are Mother Nature's Rage, and for good reason. Not only do tornadoes carry winds up to 300 miles per hour (480 km/h) winds that can level buildings and carry cars through the air 80 feet (25 m) or higher, they are also often accompanied by lighting, heavy rains (and flash floods) and tons and tons of hail. When a Tornado strikes, your every choice can mean the difference between life and death.

Torna-do's and Torna-dont's of different tornado scenarios.

  • Step 1:Don't panick. If you start panicking, other people around you will follow and panick as well. Stay calm and keep everyone distracted with a board game or if you have an MP3 player, plug yourself in and listen to music.
  • Tip 2: Immediately seek shelter. If you have a tornado shelter, don't hesitate. Take shelter and have a manual powered radio with you to listen for the all clear signal. If you don't have a storm shelter, seek safety in your basement in a small, ecnlosed space. The more walls you have around you and the storm, the more likely of your chances of survival.
